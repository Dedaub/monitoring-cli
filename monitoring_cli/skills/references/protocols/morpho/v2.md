# Morpho V2 — Vaults V2 (+ Markets V2 / "Midnight") — Topics, Selectors, Addresses

**Status:** Vaults V2 verified against live RPC on 2026-05-29 and the `morpho-org/vault-v2` repo (factory `CreateVaultV2` validated against Ethereum logs). **Markets V2 ("Midnight") is announced but NOT yet live on mainnet** as of 2026-05 — see §5. Index in [README.md](README.md); the Morpho Blue + MetaMorpho stack is [v1.md](v1.md).
**Scope:** "Morpho V2" = two pieces. **(1) Vaults V2** — a new, protocol-agnostic ERC-4626 curated-vault standard that can allocate across Morpho V1 markets, Morpho V2 markets, and (via adapters) other protocols, with an ID-based cap system and an owner/curator/allocator/sentinel role split. **(2) Markets V2 / Midnight** — an intent-based, fixed-rate / fixed-term peer-to-peer lending primitive (the successor to Morpho Blue's variable-rate markets), rolling out in phases through 2026.

Like everything in Morpho, Vaults V2 vaults and the factory are **immutable** (no proxy). A vault's *configuration* is mutable through a timelocked `submit`→`accept` governance flow, but the bytecode is not upgradeable.

> Vaults V2 is deployed on **Ethereum, Base, Polygon, Arbitrum, Optimism, Avalanche** — **not** BNB (as of 2026-05-29). The `VaultV2Factory` is byte-identical (23124 B) across chains.

### 1.3 Adapters (MorphoMarketV1Adapter / MorphoVaultV1Adapter)

Adapters are how a Vaults V2 vault routes liquidity into Morpho V1 markets (MorphoMarketV1Adapter) or MetaMorpho V1 vaults (MorphoVaultV1Adapter). They are deployed by their own factories (`MorphoMarketV1AdapterV2Factory`, `MorphoVaultV1AdapterFactory`) and emit adapter-specific allocation events; the *vault* logs `Allocate`/`Deallocate` referencing the adapter address. Detect routing by the vault's `Allocate`/`Deallocate` `adapter` field.

## 5. Markets V2 / "Midnight" — announced, NOT yet live (2026-05)

Morpho V2's lending primitive is **Midnight**: an **intent-based, fixed-rate / fixed-term** peer-to-peer credit market. Lenders/borrowers submit *intents* (offers / liquidity requests with terms); an execution engine matches them, replacing formula-based interest with market-driven rate formation. Vaults V2 is explicitly designed to allocate across both Morpho V1 markets and Midnight (V2 markets) via adapters.

> **Framing nuance (verified 2026-05-29):** Morpho's own positioning is that Midnight is **"a completely new paradigm for onchain lending, and should not be considered a 'V2' of Blue"** (Frambot). "Morpho V2" is the umbrella name (Vaults V2 + Markets V2); the standalone product brand is **Midnight**. The **Midnight whitepaper + full codebase were open-sourced on GitHub on 2026-05-28** (one day before this doc) — so source-level event/function signatures may now be derivable, but there is still no audited mainnet deployment.

**As of 2026-05-29 Midnight (Markets V2) has no finalized mainnet contract addresses** — it is rolling out in phases through 2026 following audits. There are therefore **no verified mainnet topics/selectors/addresses to monitor yet**. When it ships:
- Discover its market/intent contracts via the Morpho docs addresses page and a new adapter type registered on `MorphoRegistry`.
- Vaults V2 vaults that opt into Midnight will route through a Midnight adapter, surfaced in the vault's `Allocate`/`Deallocate` `adapter` field and `AddAdapter` events.

Update this section with verified Midnight topics/selectors/addresses once it is live. (Cross-check: variable-rate Morpho Blue markets in [v1.md](v1.md) remain fully live and are not deprecated by V2.)

## 6. Detection invariants & gotchas

1. **Vaults V2 vs MetaMorpho (V1) share ERC-4626 `Deposit`/`Withdraw` topics** (`0xdcbc1c05…`/`0xfbde797d…`) but **not `AccrueInterest`** — Vaults V2 is the 4-field `0x4dec04e7…`, MetaMorpho the 2-field `0xf66f28b4…`. Disambiguate vault type by factory provenance (`CreateVaultV2` vs `CreateMetaMorpho`) and by the 4-field AccrueInterest.
2. **`ids` in `Allocate`/`Deallocate`/cap events are abstract risk-IDs**, not Morpho market ids. The ID-cap system lets a curator cap exposure by shared risk factor (collateral, market, protocol) — `IncreaseAbsoluteCap`/`IncreaseRelativeCap` carry `(bytes32 id, bytes idData, uint256 cap)`.
3. **Governance is timelocked indirection.** Most config changes appear as `Submit(bytes4 selector, …)` then later `Accept(bytes4 selector, …)`; a `Revoke` (sentinel) cancels. To know *what* changed, decode the `selector` (e.g. `setCurator`, `setPerformanceFee`, `addAdapter`). Don't expect a direct `SetX` call in the tx that proposes it.
4. **Role split:** owner (governance) / curator (risk params) / allocator (day-to-day liquidity) / sentinel (emergency revoke). Watch `SetCurator`/`SetIsAllocator`/`SetIsSentinel` (the first two share topic0 with MetaMorpho).
5. **`forceDeallocate` is permissionless** and charges a penalty (`SetForceDeallocatePenalty`) — it is the user-facing exit when an allocator hasn't freed liquidity; `ForceDeallocate` carries `penaltyAssets`.
6. **Adapters route to other protocols.** A Vaults V2 vault is not itself a lender — it allocates through adapters (MorphoMarketV1Adapter → Morpho Blue markets, MorphoVaultV1Adapter → MetaMorpho vaults, future Midnight adapter). Follow the `adapter` address in `Allocate`/`Deallocate`.
7. **Gates** (`SetReceiveSharesGate`/`SetSendSharesGate`/`SetReceiveAssetsGate`/`SetSendAssetsGate`) implement transfer/deposit/withdraw allowlists for institutional/compliance vaults — a non-zero gate means restricted share/asset flow.
8. **BNB has no Vaults V2; Avalanche does** (but its `MorphoVaultV1AdapterFactory` was not confirmed). Always check `(chainId, factory)` existence before assuming.
